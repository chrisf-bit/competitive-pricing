## Section A - eRPD & Pricing Metrics (4 items)

### A1. What does eRPD stand for?
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) Estimated Revenue Per Day
- b) **Experienced Relative Price Difference** ✓
- c) External Rate Performance Data
- d) Effective Rate Per Discount

**Rationale:** "Experienced RPD (Experienced Relative Price Difference) is a pricing metric that reflects the prices that all our travellers experience on our platform."
**Source:** [STEERING] page 9

---

### A2. When eRPD goes DOWN, this means our prices on Booking.com are…
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) Less competitive vs Brand.com and Key OTAs
- b) **More competitive vs Brand.com and Key OTAs** ✓
- c) Higher than competitors
- d) Affecting Genius members only

**Rationale:** Lower eRPD means Booking.com is cheaper relative to Brand.com and Key OTAs based on what travellers see - i.e. more price-competitive. The 2026 goal is explicitly *to lower this metric vs last year*.
**Source:** [STEERING] page 8 ("In 2026 our goal is to improve this metric, which means to be lower vs last year")

---

### A3. eRPD combines which two underlying metrics?
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) **Public RPD and Loyal RPD** ✓
- b) Public RPD and Cross-channel RPD
- c) Loyal RPD and Genius RPD
- d) Brand.com RPD and OTA RPD

**Rationale:** "We combine both RPD views, Public and Loyal, into the Experienced RPD." The mix reflects the share of searches from non-logged-in public travellers and logged-in Genius members.
**Source:** [STEERING] page 9

---

### A4. Which of these is NOT a driver metric for eRPD?
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) Public Pricing Adoption %
- b) Genius Pricing Adoption %
- c) Competitive Partner Share
- d) **Average Daily Rate** ✓

**Rationale:** The driver-metric pyramid for eRPD includes Public RPD, Loyal RPD, Public Pricing Adoption %, Competitive Partner Share, and Genius Pricing Adoption %. ADR is not a driver metric in this framework.
**Source:** [STEERING] page 7

## Section B - On-platform vs Cross-channel (2 items)

### B1. A partner shows you data that their direct website is converting better than their Booking.com listing this month. This is information about…
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) On-platform competitiveness on Booking.com
- b) **Cross-channel competitiveness** ✓
- c) eRPD vs Key OTAs
- d) Partner's Booking.com performance

**Rationale:** Cross-channel competitiveness compares a property's performance on Booking.com to performance on other channels (their own site, other OTAs). On-platform refers only to comparison *within* Booking.com.
**Source:** [STEERING] page 4

---

### B2. Which of these can Booking.com's data definitively show? (select all that apply)
**Type:** Multi-select
- ☑ How a partner's prices compare to similar properties listed on Booking.com
- ☑ A partner's visibility and conversion **on Booking.com**
- ☐ A partner's overall business performance across all channels
- ☐ Why a competitor's website lists a different price

**Rationale:** We can speak to performance ON Booking.com (on-platform competitiveness, visibility, conversion). We cannot make claims about a partner's overall business or external pricing intent - those are off-platform and not our data to interpret.
**Source:** [STEERING] page 4 + [LEGAL] General Comms ("Advise partners specifically on their performance on Booking.com")

## Section C - Partner Freedom (3 items)

### C1. True or False: Booking.com requires partners to lower their prices when cheaper rates appear on competitor channels.
**Type:** True / False
- ☐ True
- ☑ **False**

**Rationale:** "Booking.com does not require lower prices from partners because there are cheaper rates on other online platforms." Partners can be *asked* for their best prices and reminded of the importance of staying competitive on our platform - but they are never required to lower prices.
**Source:** [STEERING] page 3 + [LEGAL] General Comms Don't

---

### C2. Which of these statements is TRUE about partner pricing strategy?
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) Partners must align their Booking.com prices with their cheapest channel
- b) **Partners are entirely free to determine their own pricing strategy** ✓
- c) Partners must apply Genius discounts to remain visible
- d) Partners with non-competitive prices receive automatic ranking penalties

**Rationale:** Verbatim from the steering principles - "Partners remain entirely free to determine their own pricing strategy." We support whatever approach they choose, whether consistent across channels or diversified.
**Source:** [STEERING] page 3 + [LEGAL] General Comms Do

---

### C3. A partner asks: *"If I don't lower my Booking.com price, will my ranking suffer?"* Which response is allowed?
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) "Yes, non-competitive partners are deprioritised in search results."
- b) "No, ranking has nothing to do with pricing - those are unrelated."
- c) **"You're free to set any price. Less competitive prices can reduce your conversion and visibility on Booking.com, but the choice is yours."** ✓
- d) "We can't discuss anything related to ranking with you directly."

**Rationale:** (a) threatens ranking - prohibited. (b) is factually misleading and undersells the impact. (d) is unhelpful evasion. (c) correctly states partner freedom AND describes the on-platform performance consequence factually, without coercion or threat.
**Source:** [LEGAL] General Comms Do/Don't + [LEGAL] Wide Parity Do ("Explain that less competitive prices can reduce their conversion and visibility on Booking.com")

## Section D - Parity Basics (3 items)

### D1. Booking.com operates in three parity regimes globally. Which list is correct?
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) Strict / Loose / None
- b) **Wide / Narrow / No-Parity** ✓
- c) Full / Partial / Open
- d) Mandatory / Optional / Free

**Source:** [LEGAL] section headings (Wide Parity Markets / Narrow Parity Markets / No-Parity Markets)

---

### D2. You are speaking with a partner in a NO-PARITY market and want to discuss their pricing. Which of these is correct?
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) You can ask them to align prices with their direct website
- b) **You should avoid using the word "parity" entirely** ✓
- c) You can require they match the cheapest OTA rate
- d) You should not discuss pricing at all

**Rationale:** In No-Parity markets the word "parity" must not be used. You can still discuss pricing - by asking for the partner's best price for Booking.com - but you cannot frame it as a parity request.
**Source:** [LEGAL] No-Parity Markets Don't ("Do not use the word 'parity' at all")

---

### D3. In a NARROW parity market, you can ask a partner to align rates with…
**Type:** Single-select multiple choice
- a) **Brand.com only** ✓
- b) Brand.com and all key OTAs
- c) The lowest competitor price across any channel
- d) Whatever achieves price parity across the entire market

**Rationale:** Narrow parity covers the partner's own direct site only. Asking for alignment with other OTAs is explicitly prohibited in Narrow markets.
**Source:** [LEGAL] Narrow Parity Markets Do/Don't

## Section E - Safe vs Unsafe Phrases (Bucketing exercise)

**Mechanic:** The learner sees each phrase and drags it into one of three buckets:
- ✅ **SAFE** - can use as-is
- ⛔ **UNSAFE** - must not say
- ✏️ **RE-PHRASE** - the underlying intent is OK but the wording isn't

**Note for SMEs:** The third bucket (RE-PHRASE) is a teaching device - gives the system somewhere to put borderline statements with a coaching tip. Flag if you'd prefer a binary Safe/Unsafe split.

---

### E1. *"Booking.com requires you to match the prices on your Brand.com site or your visibility may be reduced."*
**Bucket:** ⛔ UNSAFE
**Why:** Two violations in one sentence - frames pricing as a *requirement* (we never require) and *threatens visibility* in exchange for compliance.
**Source:** [LEGAL] General Comms Don't (×2)

---

### E2. *"Less competitive prices can reduce your conversion and visibility on Booking.com."*
**Bucket:** ✅ SAFE
**Why:** Verbatim approved Wide Parity phrasing. Factual, on-platform-only, no threat or requirement.
**Source:** [LEGAL] Wide Parity Markets Do (verbatim)

---

### E3. *"What's the best price you'd be willing to make available to Booking.com to remain competitive on the platform?"*
**Bucket:** ✅ SAFE
**Why:** Verbatim approved No-Parity phrasing. Asks for *best price for Booking.com* - does not require alignment with anything external.
**Source:** [LEGAL] No-Parity Markets Do (verbatim)

---

### E4. *"Partners with non-competitive prices receive ranking penalties."*
**Bucket:** ⛔ UNSAFE
**Why:** "Penalties" + "ranking" together is a direct threat. Even if the underlying performance signal is real, this framing is prohibited.
**Source:** [LEGAL] General Comms Don't ("Do not write anything that could be interpreted as pressuring parity or threatening ranking changes")

---

### E5. *"Pricing is one of several performance drivers, alongside reviews, content, and availability."*
**Bucket:** ✅ SAFE
**Why:** Frames pricing as one of multiple drivers - correct positioning. Avoids over-emphasising price as a sole lever.
**Source:** [LEGAL] General Comms Do (verbatim)

---

### E6. *"Partners are completely free to choose their own pricing and distribution strategy."*
**Bucket:** ✅ SAFE
**Why:** Verbatim approved phrasing. Should be used proactively to reinforce partner freedom, especially when discussing pricing performance.
**Source:** [LEGAL] General Comms Do (verbatim)

---

### E7. *"You should switch off other OTAs and route all your inventory through Booking.com."*
**Bucket:** ⛔ UNSAFE
**Why:** Recommending a partner switch off other OTAs is explicitly prohibited - it dictates external distribution strategy.
**Source:** [LEGAL] Wide Parity Markets Don't (verbatim)

---

### E8. *"I can see your prices on other platforms are lower - could you tell me whether that's intentional and part of your strategy?"*
**Bucket:** ✅ SAFE *(in No-Parity context, **reactively** only)*
**Why:** A neutral, fact-finding question is allowed reactively if you've noticed a discrepancy. Importantly - this is reactive, not proactive: the topic should already have surfaced.
**Source:** [LEGAL] No-Parity Markets Do (verbatim)
